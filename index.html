~ the why ~

This project is dedicated to promoting positive study skills among the student body, ideally in a manner which is applicable to any other institution, yet which is nonetheless attuned to our own insofar as its particular interviews are concerned. In my academic experience up to this point, I have seen many of my peers struggle with keeping up with their work in the classroom in a manner which does not in and of itself further harm their scholastic endeavors.


Students are massively impacted by lack of access to resources which promote positive time management and study skills because their time is implicitly limited. If a student is expected to self-search and develop positive habits without any external support, they may succeed - but in doing so, they will be obliged to focus less on school work instead. By providing a resource with opinions from their peers, teachers, and other experts, though, they can find strategies within a system that promotes growth without causing even more stress to students or their schedules.


Students have a hard time finding manageable and low-stress ways to study while keeping up with their other obligations. This project aims to develop a codified resource which can offer suggestions and expedite individual exploration of potential strategies for time management and study - especially exam prep.
